Note that Keith and Tim could have avoided sending a third and fourth message. In fact, Keith, and later Tim, could have refrained from disrupting the entire group to thank each other!

People who act like this usually try to be polite, avoiding being rude, as one would do in person or on the phone - and that's fine! But we're in 2024, and chat is neither. Most people don't want non-relevant messages in their chat. So, despite the best intentions, you're basically filling the chat with unnecessary messages, which is a waste of time (and a bit annoying).

The same goes for:

  • "Thanks a lot!"
  • "thanksss"
  • "Thank you all"
  • "tysm"
  • "hey, thanks"
  • etc.

If you think you're being rude by asking a question and just saying "Thanks," you can finish your message with as many thanks as you want.

For example:

  • "hey, how's it going? any idea about the deadline for that thing? thanks a lot"
  • "Hope everything's going well. I have a question, when you have a second, thanks in advance"
  • "hey, if you're not busy, could you update the NFRs? thanks"
  • etc.

It might seem trivial, but saying thanks before the response avoids many other thanks. If another person had the same question, reading the answer would make them want to thank you. Bam, just use a reaction üëç and it's all sorted. No unnecessary notifications.
